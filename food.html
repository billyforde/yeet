What Is There To Eat?

    Not only does Orlando have some killer attractions, it has killer food too. Shockingly, Orlando has a variety of known culinary geniuses under its belt. So grab your napkins and your bottomless appetite, because here are some great restaurants to give you the Orlando culinary experience!


  • Kadence: Japanese Food
    Price: $75 for a Sunday lunch of raw and cooked dishes and $55 for lunch on certain weekdays.
    Days Open/Hours: Mon through Wed closed, Thurs through Sat 4-7pm, Sun 12 through 2:30pm.
    Takeout only.

  • Kres Chophouse: Steakhouse
    Hours: Mon through Thurs 11:10 through 11pm, Fri 11:30am through 12am, Sat 4 through 12am, Sun 5 through 11pm.
    Price: Varies
    Dine-in and Takeout.

  • Se7enBites: Lunch and Breakfast Bakery
    Hours: Mon-Wed closed, Thurs-Fri 8am-3:30pm, Sat-Sun 9am-3pm
    Prices: Varies
    Dine-in and Curbside Pickup.

  • Prato: Looking for a drop of italian culture to please your taste buds? Look no further than Prato, a nice little italian restaurant, with not only an innovative theme, but an innovative food selection as well. Their authentic, wood-burning oven will bring you savory pizzas and house made pastas to die for.
    Price: You can find their menu and list of prices here.
    Hours: Mon-Wed 5:30-11pm, Wed-Sat 11:30pm-11:pm, and Sun 11:30pm-10pm

  • Mrs. Potato: If you're from Idaho, you already see potatoes everywhere, but if you get a little home-sick at the NLC, there is nowhere better than the brazilian restaurant, Mrs. Potato.
    Price: You can find their menu and list of prices here.
    Hours: Mon-thu 11am-10pm, Fri-Sat 11am-11am

  • Dragonfly-Robata Grill and Sushi: If you're looking to expand your culinary experience, look no further than asian cuisine. Dragonfly is a multiple award winning restaurant, eager to give new customers a taste of their tradition.
    Price: You can find their menu and list of prices here
    Hours: Wed 5-9pm, Thu 5-9pm, Fri 5-10pm, Sat 5-10pm, Sun 5-9pm, Mon 5-9pm, Tue 5-9pm

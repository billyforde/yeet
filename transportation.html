The SunRail™

    Arguably, the best way to get around most of Florida, let alone Orlando, on the weekdays, is the SunRail metro. Allow me to reinstate the fact that the SunRail, while very efficient, only runs from Monday to Friday. NLC is most likely going to cut into the weekend, which is why we have LYMMO. But we will discuss LYMMO later, back to the SunRail. SunRail has a total of four stations in Orlando--AdventHealth Station, LYNX Central Station, Church Street Station, and Orlando Health / Amtrak Station, which we will provide the locations of in a map here. The only costs a one way visitor like yourselves should worry about for the SunRail are $2 plus an additional dollar for every county you cross.

    The site we just linked will also cover whatever we may leave off of this particular web site, in case you still want to know more about the SunRail. The schedules for the SunRail obviously may be different by the time NLC comes around, so be sure to check these on the way to Orlando!

LYMMO™

    The LYMMO Downtown Circulator is a fare-free circulator that provides downtown Orlando visitors with quick access to restaurants, retail, entertainment, offices and services. LYMMO operates on four different routes in partially dedicated lanes with transit signal priority. But this fun little bus system won't stop there, it is currently being transformed into an electric fleet of some sort, which will make it cleaner, and easier to manage. The map of all of LYMMO’s pick up spots and routes can be found here. These are the times that LYMMO functions:

  • Monday through Friday: 6:00 a.m. to 10:45 p.m.
  • Saturday: 10:00 a.m. to 10:45 p.m.
  • Sunday: 10:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m.

   This is another very good free method of transportation for all of the important people going to the NLC, because it is completely free, it has flexible times for pick-up, and it's very safe and clean, with LYMMO, you can make it to your destination without the hassle of spending a dime or planning ahead! For this reason, it is a prime suggestion for seeing Orlando in its fullest.

Billy Forde, Dilyn Graefe, Brad Pearson
